# Energy Saving Tips / نصائح لتوفير الطاقة

## Air Conditioning / المكيف

- Typical power draw: 1.5 - 3.5 kW depending on size and efficiency.
- Each 1 degree Celsius lower on thermostat adds approximately 8% more consumption.
- Setting to 24 degrees instead of 20 degrees saves approximately 30% on AC costs.
- Pre-cool your home during off-peak hours (05:00-14:00) if on TOU tariff. The cooled mass helps maintain temperature into peak hours.
- Clean filters monthly. Dirty filters increase consumption by 15-25%.
- Close curtains and blinds on sun-facing windows during afternoon hours (14:00-17:00). Direct sunlight significantly increases cooling load.
- Use ceiling fans alongside AC. Fans allow raising the thermostat 2-3 degrees while maintaining comfort. A fan uses only 0.05-0.1 kW compared to 1.5-3.5 kW for AC.
- Ensure doors and windows are properly sealed. Air leaks force the compressor to work harder.

## Water Heater / سخان الماء

- Typical power draw: 2 - 4 kW. One of the highest single-appliance loads in a home.
- Use a timer to heat water during off-peak hours (05:00-14:00). Insulated tanks keep water hot for hours.
- Reduce thermostat from 60 degrees to 50 degrees Celsius. Saves approximately 10% and reduces standby losses.
- A 2.5 kW heater running 2 hours at peak: 2.5 x 2 x 160 = 800 fils.
- Same heater at off-peak: 2.5 x 2 x 108 = 540 fils. Savings: 260 fils per day.
- Consider a solar water heater. Jordan gets 300+ sunny days per year. Payback period: 2-3 years.
- Insulate hot water pipes to reduce heat loss during distribution.

## EV Charging / شحن السيارة الكهربائية

- Home Level 2 charger: 3-7 kW, typical full charge 4-8 hours.
- Peak charging (17:00-23:00) at 160 fils/kWh is the most expensive option.
- Off-peak charging (05:00-14:00) at 108 fils/kWh saves 32.5%.
- Use your car's built-in charge timer to automatically start charging at off-peak hours (e.g., set to start at 05:00).
- A 7kW charger for 4 hours: peak = 4,480 fils vs off-peak = 3,024 fils. Daily savings: 1,456 fils (1.46 JOD). Monthly savings: approximately 43.7 JOD.
- If your utility supports TOU metering, consider a dedicated EV meter for lower rates.

## Lighting / الإضاءة

- LED bulbs use 75% less energy than incandescent and last 25 times longer.
- LED: 10W vs incandescent: 60W for equivalent brightness.
- 20 bulbs in a home: switching from incandescent to LED saves approximately 1 kWh per day = 30 kWh per month.
- At Tier 3 rates (86 fils/kWh), that is 2,580 fils (2.58 JOD) monthly savings.
- Use motion sensors in hallways and bathrooms to avoid leaving lights on.

## General Tips / نصائح عامة

- Unplug phone chargers, TV boxes, and other devices on standby. Standby power (phantom load) accounts for 5-10% of a typical electricity bill.
- Run washing machines and dishwashers during off-peak hours and always with full loads. Half-loads use nearly the same energy.
- Use a power strip with a switch for entertainment centers. One switch cuts all standby power.
- In summer, close curtains during peak sun hours (11:00-16:00) to reduce cooling needs by up to 30%.

## Spike Detection and Investigation / كشف والتحقيق في الارتفاعات

- A spike is when power draw exceeds 2x the normal level for that hour of day.
- Common spike causes: water heater at peak hours, EV charging at peak, multiple ACs running simultaneously, electric oven during peak.
- Smart meters record every 15 minutes, allowing pinpointing of exact spike times.
- Investigation approach: identify spike time, ask what appliances were running, calculate cost impact, suggest schedule changes.
