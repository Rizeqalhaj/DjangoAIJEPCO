# Electricity Billing FAQ

## How do I read my bill?

Your electricity bill consists of:
- Fixed monthly charge: 500 fils (single phase) or 1,500 fils (three phase)
- Energy charge: Based on progressive tiers (the more you use, the higher the rate per kWh)
- Total bill = Fixed charge + Energy charge

## Why is my bill so high?

Common reasons for high bills:
- Running AC at low temperatures (each degree lower adds 8% consumption)
- Water heater running during peak hours
- EV charging during peak hours (17:00-23:00)
- Devices on standby mode consuming phantom power
- Dirty AC filters (increases consumption 15-25%)
- Air leaks from windows or doors increasing cooling load

## How can I lower my bill?

- Set AC to 24 degrees instead of 20 (saves approximately 30%)
- Use a timer for your water heater to run during off-peak hours (05:00-14:00)
- Charge your EV between 5 AM and 2 PM for the cheapest rates
- Switch incandescent bulbs to LED (saves 75% on lighting)
- Unplug devices you are not using (standby = 5-10% of your bill)

## How do the tariff tiers work?

The tariff is progressive: the first 160 kWh costs 33 fils/kWh, then rates increase with each tier. Above 1,000 kWh you pay 265 fils/kWh. This means even saving 50 kWh can drop you to a lower tier and save more than you expect.

## How do I dispute my bill?

- Contact JEPCO customer service
- Request a meter inspection
- Keep monthly meter readings for comparison
- You can request a payment plan for high bills

## What are the peak hours?

- Off-Peak: 5:00 AM - 2:00 PM (cheapest rates)
- Partial Peak: 2:00 PM - 5:00 PM and 11:00 PM - 5:00 AM
- Peak: 5:00 PM - 11:00 PM (most expensive rates)

Try to run heavy appliances (washing machine, water heater, EV charger) during off-peak hours.

## What is a consumption spike?

A spike is when your power usage exceeds 2 times the normal level for that time of day. Common causes include running a water heater, EV charger, or multiple ACs simultaneously during peak hours. Your smart meter records every 15 minutes, allowing precise identification of when spikes occur.
